Eggplant Rollatini

Adapted from Ellie Krieger, Accent Health
Yield: 4 Servings

Ingredients

  • olive oil spray
  • 1 large eggplant
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 teaspoon freshly ground black pepper
  • 2 cups (whole jar) marinara sauce
  • 1 cup (half a 15 oz. package) part-skim ricotta cheese
  • 1 large egg, lightly beaten
  • 1/2 cup lightly packed chopped fresh arugula
  • 1 teaspoon freshly grated lemon zest
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 12 large basil leaves (whole package)
  • 2 tablespoons finely grated fresh parmesan

Directions

  1. Wash and peel the eggplant. Slice crosswise into thin disks, about 1/4-inch thick.
  2. Turn on the oven's broiler.
  3. Line two baking trays with foil and coat each with olive oil spray.
  4. Arrange the eggplant in one layer and spray with oil.
  5. Season with 1/4 teaspoon of salt and 1/4 teaspoon of pepper.
  6. Broil trays on high, about 5 inches from the flame, flipping the eggplant halfway through, until golden brown on both sides and tender, about 5 minutes total. Let cool.
  7. Turn the oven to 375 degrees.
  8. Whisk together the ricotta, egg, arugula, lemon zest, and garlic, with the remaining salt and pepper — 1/4 teaspoon each.
  9. Spoon half of the marinara sauce into the bottom of a 9-inch by 13-inch baking dish.
  10. Arrange half the eggplant slices in a layer on top of the marinara sauce.
  11. Spoon a layer of ricotta mixture over the eggplant.
  12. Arrange the basil leaves in a layer on top of the ricotta mixture.
  13. Arrange the other half of the eggplant slices in a layer on top of the basil leaves.
  14. Spoon the remaining sauce over the eggplant slices.
  15. Bake uncovered in middle of oven, until sauce is bubbling and heated through, 25 to 30 minutes.
  16. Sprinkle with grated parmesan and serve.
