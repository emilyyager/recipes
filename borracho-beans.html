Borracho Beans

Ingredients

  • 1 pound dry pinto beans
  • three 14.5oz cans chicken stock
  • 1 tablespoon salt
  • 1/2 tablespoon ground black pepper
  • 8 slices of thick-sliced bacon
  • 1 white onion, diced
  • 3 cloves garlic, chopped
  • 3 jalapeños, stemmed, seeded and chopped
  • 1 teaspoon cumin seeds
  • 1 teaspoon dried oregano
  • 1/2 cup fresh cilantro, chopped
  • 1 tablespoon chili powder or 1 tbs guajillo chile powder (ground chiles)]
  • 1 teaspoon chopped bay leaves
  • 5 fresh mediumsized tomatoes (14 oz) diced (or 1 14oz can of diced tomatoes)
  • one 16oz bottle of beer – Dos Equis
  • 3 sprigs epazote (optional)
  • What is in HEB bean seasoning?

Directions

  1. Sort beans to remove stones. Rinse. Soak beans in a large pot of water overnight.
  2. Drain beans, and refill the pot with chicken stock and enough water to cover the beans with 2 inches of liquid.
  3. Season with salt and pepper. Cover, and bring to a boil. Reduce heat to medium-low, cover, and cook for 1 1/2 hours. Stir the beans occasionally through out the entire cooking process to make sure they do not burn or stick to the bottom of the pot.
  4. Cook bacon, drain on paper towels and chop when cooled.
  5. Sauté cook onion, garlic, jalapenos, cumin. Add oregano and cilantro to skillet, cook until fragrant, about 2 minutes.
  6. After 11/2 hours of cooking beans, add bacon, onion, garlic, jalapeno, cumin, oregano, cilantro. Also add chili powder, bay leaves, tomatoes and beer.
  7. Continue to cook for an additional 0:30 min –1hr or until beans are tender
  8. Serve immediately or refrigerate overnight.
