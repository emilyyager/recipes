Grilled Beet Stacks With Chèvre and Pecans

Source: Meredith Bethune for Edible Austin (Summer 2013)
Serves 4 as an appetizer

Ingredients

  • 4 large beets, peeled
  • 4 ounces chèvre, room temperature
  • 1 Tablespoon milk
  • 2 Tablespoons chopped pecans
  • 1 Tablespoon minced chives
  • 2 teaspoons fresh thyme
  • 1 teaspoon minced fresh rosemary
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground black pepper
  • olive oil
  • salt and pepper, to taste
  • juice of 1 lemon

Directions

  1. Place the beets in a pot and cover with salted water. Bring to a rapid boil, then reduce the heat to a simmer. Cook the beets until they can be pierced with a fork&mash;about 10 to 15 minutes.
  2. Drain and set aside until cool enough to handle.
  3. In a bowl, combine chèvre, milk, pecans, herbs and black pepper. Stir until well combined and set aside.
  4. Slice each beet into 3 or 4 horizontal rounds about 1/4- to 1/2-inch thick.
  5. In a small bowl, combine the beet slice with a bit of olive oil, salt, pepper and the lemon juice. Toss to coat evenly.
  6. Using tongs, place the beet slices on a hot grill (avoiding the hottest spots) and cook for about 10 minutes per side. Remove the beets to a plate and let cool.
  7. To assemble the stacks, spread about 1 1/2 teaspoons of the chèvre mixture on top of one beet slice. Place another beet slice on top, and then repeat until there are two or three layers of cheese and all four stacks are assembled.
