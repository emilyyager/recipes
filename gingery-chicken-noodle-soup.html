Gingery Chicken Noodle Soup

Adapted from a recipe by Mayo Clinic

Ingredients

  • 3 ounces of dried soba noodles
  • 1 Tablespoon olive oil
  • 1 large yellow onion, chopped
  • 1 Tablespoon ginger, peeled and minced
  • 1 carrot, peeled and finely chopped
  • 1 clove garlic, minced
  • 4 cups chicken stock or broth
  • 2 Tablespoons soy sauce
  • 1 pound skinless, boneless chicken breasts, chopped
  • 1 cup shelled edamame
  • 1 cup plain soy milk
  • 1/4 cup cilantro, chopped

Directions

  1. Bring a saucepan 3/4 full of water to a boil. Add the soba noodles and cook until just tender — about 4 minutes. Drain and set aside until needed.
  2. In a large saucepan, heat the olive oil over medium heat.
  3. Add the onion and sauté until soft and translucent — about 4 minutes.
  4. Add the ginger and carrot and sauté for 1 minute.
  5. Add the garlic and sauté for 30 seconds. Do not let the garlic brown.
  6. Add the stock and soy sauce and bring to a boil.
  7. Add the chicken and edamame and return to boil.
  8. Reduce heat to medium-low and simmer until the chicken is cooked and the edamame are tender — about 4 minutes.
  9. Add the soba noodles and soy milk. Cook until heated through. Do not let boil.
  10. Remove from heat and stir in cilantro. Ladle into warmed individual bowls and serve immediately.
