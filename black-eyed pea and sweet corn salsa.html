Haven’t Made Black-Eyed Pea and Sweet Corn Salsa Serves 8

Ingredients

  • 1 cup dried black-eyed peas, picked over and rinsed, soaked overnight and drained
  • 3 cups of water
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1 Tablespoon of olive oil
  • 1/2 yellow onion, chopped
  • 1/2 green bell pepper, seeded and chopped
  • 1 cup fresh corn kernels (cut from about 2 ears corn)
  • 1 tomato, seeded and diced
  • 1 clove garlic, minced
  • 1 lime, squeezed for juice and grated for zest
  • 1 Tablespoon white wine vinegar or sherry vinegar
  • 3 Tablespoons chopped fresh cilantro
  • 1/4 teaspoon pepper

Directions

In a large saucepan over high heat, combine the peas, water, and 1/4 teaspoon of salt. Bring to a boil. Reduce heat to low, cover partially, and simmer until peas are tender, about 45 minutes. Drain the peas, rinse with cool water, drain again, and transfer to a large bowl to cool. In a large nonstick frying pan, heat the olive oil over medium-high heat. Add the onion and bell pepper and sauté until softened, about 4 minutes. Add the corn, tomato and garlic and sauté until the tomato is softened and the corn is tender-crisp, about 4 minutes. Add the corn mixture to the peas along with the lime zest and juice, vinegar, cilantro, the remaining 1/4 teaspoon salt and the pepper. Toss to mix. Serve immediately, or cover and refrigerate for up to 2 days.
