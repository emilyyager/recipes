Basque Chicken Stew With Tomatoes, Potatoes, and Other Yummy Things

Source: Name, Delicious House, adapted from Paul Williams

Ingredients

  • 2 large chicken breasts
  • 1/4 lb bacon, don't leave it out!
  • 2 medium yellow onions
  • 2 red bell peppers (or 1 red and 1 green)
  • 1 can fire roasted diced tomatoes
  • 2 cups chicken stock, preferably homemade
  • 2 cups small new potatoes, peeled and chopped into large chunks
  • 1 cup white wine
  • 1 bunch fresh Italian parsley
  • 1 pinch cayenne pepper (about 1/4 teaspoon)
  • kosher salt
  • freshly ground pepper
  • olive oil

Directions

  1. Preheat the oven to 350 degrees. Brush olive oil over 2 chicken breasts, and sprinkle liberally with kosher salt and freshly ground black pepper. Roast in oven 15 to 20 minutes until cooked through. Remove from the oven, and let cool.
  2. Dice Chicken.
  3. Cut 2 red bell peppers in half lengthwise, and remove the stem and seeds. Roast in the oven until tough skins are blackened, about 20 minutes. Remove from oven and let cool.
  4. Remove skins for peppers, and slice into long strips.
  5. Cut 1/4 lb bacon into small strips. Sauté in a stockpot until crispy. Remove from pan, reserving the bacon grease.
  6. Chop two medium onions into long strips. Saute in the reserved bacon fat until softened and translucent.
  7. Add the following to the pot:
    • bacon
    • chicken
    • sliced pepper
    • 2 cups chicken stock
    • 1 can fire roasted diced tomatoes
    • 1 cup white wine (have always omitted)
    • 1/4 cup chopped Italian parsley
    • and 2 cups peeled, roughly chopped new potatoes.
    • 1 pinch cayenne pepper (about 1/4 teaspoon)
  8. Bring to a boil. Reduce heat to low, and let simmer 1-2 hours, until potatoes are soft. Tastes even better the second day. Enjoy!
